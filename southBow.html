Company Overview

South Bow Corporation is a North American energy infrastructure company focused on the transportation and storage of crude oil.

It was formed through the spinoff of TC Energy’s Liquids Pipelines business, effective October 1, 2024.

Headquartered in Calgary, Alberta, with operations in both Canada and the U.S.

Key Assets

Keystone Pipeline System

  • ~4,324 km (2,687 miles) pipeline from Hardisty, Alberta to Steele City, Nebraska.
  • Extends to Cushing, Oklahoma and Gulf Coast (Houston & Port Arthur).
  • Capacity: ~610,000 barrels per day.
  • Connected to U.S. Midwest & Gulf Coast refineries.

Grand Rapids Pipeline System

  • 460 km pipeline supporting bitumen production.
  • Located north of Fort McMurray.

White Spruce Pipeline

  • Feeder line into Grand Rapids.

Cushing & Houston Terminals

  • Combined ~4.7 million barrels of storage capacity.

Port Neches Link

  • Connects to Motiva’s terminal infrastructure.

Pain Point - Pipeline Integrity

The Keystone Pipeline, operated by South Bow Corporation, has experienced a total of 23 spills since it commenced operations in 2010.

Notable Incidents

December 2022 – Kansas

Approximately 14,000 barrels of crude oil spilled into Mill Creek, marking the largest onshore oil spill in the U.S. in nearly a decade.

October 2019 – North Dakota

An estimated 4,515 barrels were released near Edinburg.

November 2017 – South Dakota

Around 6,592 barrels spilled near Amherst.

April 2025 – North Dakota

The most recent spill involved approximately 3,500 barrels near Fort Ransom.

Key Implications & Considerations

The Keystone Pipeline is a critical asset for South Bow, contributing approximately 90% of the company's earnings in 2024. However, the recurring incidents have raised concerns about the pipeline's integrity.

Supply Chain

Unlike manufacturing or retail, their supply chain is asset-heavy, risk-sensitive, and regulated — and failure in even a small part (like a valve or pump delay) can halt operations.

Long Lead Times for Critical Equipment: Specialized components (e.g., pipeline valves, pressure pumps) often have 6-12+ month lead times. Delays can impact maintenance or expansion schedules.

Reactive Vendor Management: Vendors are not always evaluated on risk, performance, or delivery history, which increases operational risk.

Poor Supply Chain Visibility Across Remote Sites: Many field locations and terminals are in remote areas (e.g., Alberta oil sands or Texas Gulf Coast), making it difficult to track deliveries, warehousing, and crew logistics.
